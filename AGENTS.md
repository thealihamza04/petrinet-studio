The design style we are following is a fusion of **Swiss International Style** and **Riso-Graph Editorial Aesthetics**. Here is a breakdown in simple terms:

## 1. The Layout (Swiss International)
* **12-Column Grid:** Everything is organized on a strict mathematical grid. This creates a sense of order and professional stability.
* **Visual Hierarchy:** We use dramatic differences in size. Headers are massive and bold, while metadata is tiny and tucked into corners.
* **Asymmetry:** Instead of centering everything, we use "white space" (empty areas) as a design element to guide the eye.

## 2. The Color & Texture (Riso-Swiss)
* **High-Contrast Palette:** We use a limited set of "spot colors"—specifically **Riso Pink (#ff4f9a)** and **Deep Blue (#006c84)**—against a warm "Paper" background.
* **Multiplication:** Where colors overlap, they "multiply" (darken), mimicking the look of physical ink layers on a printing press.
* **Grainy Texture:** A subtle digital "noise" or grain is applied over everything to remove the clinical, "too-perfect" feel of modern websites.

## 3. Typography
* **Neo-Grotesque:** We use clean, bold sans-serif fonts (**Inter**) for technical data and headers to emphasize modernism.
* **Editorial Serif:** We use elegant, italicized serifs (**Playfair Display**) for "human" touches, like quotes or artistic emphasis.

## 4. Interaction & Motion
* **Magnetic Cursor:** The mouse is replaced by a custom dot that reacts to elements on the screen, creating a tactile feel.
* **Staggered Reveals:** Elements don’t just appear; they slide up into place one by one (cascading) to create a sense of storytelling.
* **Brutalist Borders:** Sharp, 2px black borders are used for interactive blocks (like the Petri Net canvases) to give them a "blueprint" or "technical manual" vibe.

**In short:** It’s a style that looks like a high-end architecture magazine from the 1960s, but functions with the smooth motion of 2026 web technology.
